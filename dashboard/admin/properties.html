About Sedgwick County Squeeze

I built this tool as a way to visualize increases (and the rare decreases) in property appraisal values. I was quite shocked to see my own property increase by 20% this past year.

All the information here is gathered from public information on the Sedgwick County website. There is no proprietary or private information contained here.

I built this on my own free time and decided to make it free for the community. That being said, it is running on a free tier in Cloudflare.

I have open-sourced the code for it in case anyone wants to build their own version. You can find the source code here:
https://github.com/tylermiranda/SCS

Tips for Contesting Your Appraisal

  • Check for Data Errors: Ensure the county has the correct square footage, bedroom/bathroom counts, and basement finish percentage. If the county's data is wrong, providing the correct floor plans can immediately lower your valuation.
  • Analyze Comparable Sales: Look at the "Comparable Sales" section in this report. Are those houses truly comparable to yours in terms of age, condition, and location? If not, find better comps from recent sales in your neighborhood.
  • Document Maintenance Issues: The county assumes your home is in "average" condition for its age. If you have significant deferred maintenance, structural issues, or need a new roof, take detailed photos and contractor estimates to your hearing.
  • Bring an Independent Appraisal: If you recently refinanced or bought the home, a fee appraisal from a licensed appraiser within the last 12 months is often the strongest evidence you can provide.
  • The Process: The informal hearing is a conversation, not a court case. Be polite, stick to the facts and data, and avoid arguing about the taxes themselves—the hearing is strictly about the market value of the property.
